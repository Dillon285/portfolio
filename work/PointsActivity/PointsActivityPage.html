Identified issues

The current state of the date selector was not optimal as it required users to navigate through two different menus to select a singular month or a custom date range - which included two seperate calendar drop-downs

Streamlined selections

The date selector was redesigned to allow for more practical date ranges to be selected quickly and easily alongside the integration & showcase of a custom date range selector within a single menu

Existing components

Leveraged components and behaviors from the Trip Search Widget’s date selector, enabling easier technical implementation and ensuring developers were already familiar with how date selection functionality would behave

Search for Points Activity

The implementation of a search functionality was recognised as important due to the need for members to filter by more than just date. This was especially important for members with large amounts of activity who wanted to view just a specific activity or partner they earned & burned points with

Filtering Behaviour

The introduction of a new feature, especially one as complicated as searching meant that many behavioural elements had to be considered to ensure ease of use. This included how someone searches, how do the results show and what the export activity function would do when search is active

Competitive & Concept analysis

To better understanding of how search functionality could be implemented, competitive analysis was conducted to see how other Fintech companies such as banks approach searching within transactions. This was followed by concept design where ideas from analysis were explored and evaluated based on the needs of QBR members
